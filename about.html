Spirited Away is a Japanese animated film written and directed by Hayao Miyazaki. The film is produced by Toshio Suzuki and distributed by Toho.

The film was released in Japan in July 2001 with a budget of 17 million dollars and became the most successful film in Japanese history grossing over 365 million dollar worldwide.

Tale of the fanciful adventures of a ten-year-old girl named Chihiro, who discovers a secret world when she and her family get lost and venture through a hillside tunnel. When her parents undergo a mysterious transformation, Chihiro must fend for herself as she encounters strange spirits, assorted creatures and a grumpy sorceress who seeks to prevent her from returning to the human world.

8.6/10 stars on IMDB, voted by 577.985 people and the first hand-drawn and non-english film to win the Academy Awards for Best Animated Feature at the 75th Academy Awards.
